Original Coffee Forms

The original forms of coffee were simple, rustic preparations that highlight how people first discovered and enjoyed the plant. Early coffee drinkers in Ethiopia are believed to have consumed coffee cherries by chewing them or mixing them with animal fat to create an energizing paste. As coffee culture spread to Yemen, people began roasting and brewing the beans to make a drink called *qahwa*, which is considered one of the first true coffee beverages. These early forms laid the foundation for the many brewing methods and traditions that would develop around the world.
